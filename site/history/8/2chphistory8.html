From Trade to Territory: The Company Establishes Power

Company got exclusive right-

  • East india company got a charter from queen elizabeth 1 which gave it a exclusive right to trade with east. With these power it purchased cheap goods and sold them expensive in europe.
  • However the royal charter is can not be appllied on other european powers. Other european powers such as french,portuguese,dutch etc also had a presence in east. That led to competion,battle,blockade of ships between them. Companies made fortified trading posts and trade is guarded with weapons.

East india company in bengal-

In 1651 first english factory was set up at banks of hugli river. As merchants are started settling near english factory for good trade, by 1696 british started building fort around the factory.

Shortly after, british got zamindari rights over three villages by bribing mughal officials. Kalikata was one of those villages, which later grew into city Calcutta.

Aurangzeb issued a farman granting only the company the right to trade duty free. British missused this privilage. Officials of the company doing business on the side were expected to pay duty, which they refused, causing loss of revenue for mughals.

Trade to battles-

After death of aurangzed, the bengal nawabs asserted thier power. Murshid Quli Khan followed by Alivardi Khan then Sirajuddaulah were the nawabs of bengal.

They were strong rulers, and opposed many of the company's wrong practices. These conflicts led to battle of plassey.

Battle of plassey(1757)-

After death of alivardi khan, sirajuddaulah became nawab. Worried company officials tried to put a puppet ruler in place of the nawab. Sirajuddaulah demanded not to interfere in political affairs, stop factory fortification and pay the revenues.

After failed negotiations, nawab attacked factory with 30000 soldiers at kassimbazar captured officals,warehouse and blockaded english ships. Then captured company fort at calcutta.

After defeat at calcutta, british send solders from madras under command of robert clive. After failure of prolonged neotiations with nawab, in 1757 robert clive led company's army against nawab at plassey. Company's army got victory and sirajuddaulah got assasinated.

The battle of plassey became the first major victory of company won in india. After victory the company put a puppet ruler named Mir Jafar in place of Sirajuddaulah. Mir jafar was one of the commanders of sirajuddaulah. Robert clive won his support by promising to make him nawab after crushing sirajuddaulah. In exchange, the force led by mir jafar didnot fought the battle. This was one of the main reasons for the defeat of sirajuddaulah.

Battle of buxar(1764)-

Puppet rulers were not always cooperative to the company because they also have to maintain basic dignity and sovereignty to get get respect from their subjects. Mir jafar protested, company put another puppet in place of him named Mir qasim. When Mir qasim protested, he was defeated by Mir jafar with the help of company, in battle of buxar.

Diwan of provinces of Bengal-

After death of Mir jaffar in 1765, the Mughal emperor appointed the company as the Diwan of provices of bengal. It allowed the company to use the revenue resources of bengal. The revenues were so vast that it could finance company expenses like purchase of goods, maintenance of troops, fortification of company factories.

Due to huge revenue, the company didn't need to import gold and silver from britain for its trade. The revenue covered all the expenses.

Company officials became NABOBS-

Nabob is an anglicised term of the word nawab. After battle of buxar company officals earned huge amounts of money and property. Although many officials died early deaths due to war or diseases, those who managed to return to Britain with wealth, lived a comfortable life in britain. They led flashy lives and flauted their riches. They were seen as social climbers in british society and ridiculed in plays and cartoons.

COMPANY RULE EXPANDS

The tactic that company used to annex indian kingdoms was various political, economic and diplomatic methords. Company appointed its workers in various indian states. The workers were political or commmercial agents and their job was to propagate the interests of the company. Using workers company interfered in internal affairs in indian states, deciding the successors of the states etc.

Subsidiary alliance-

Under this alliance indian rulers were not allowed to have their independent army and they ware to be protected by the army of the company. In exchenge of protection they have to pay money to the company. If a ruler was failed to pay the expense, then part of their territory was taken by company as penalty.

Tipu Sultan - The "Tiger of Mysore"-

Mysore was a powerful state under leadership of Haider Ali (ruled 1761-1782) and his son Tipu Sultan (ruled 1782-1799). Profitable malabar coast was under Mysore where in 1785 Tipu sultan stopped the export of sandalwood, pepper and cardamom and not allowed local merchants to trade with company.

Tipu had close relationship with french, due to which british fought 4 wars with mysore (1767-69, 1780-84, 1790-92 and 1799) and won the last war- the battle of Seringapatam. In the last war tipu died defending his capital seringapatam. After his death, Mysore was placed under the ruling dynasty of Wodeyars and subsidiary alliance was imposed on the state.

War with the Marathas-

After Third Battle of Panipat, Maratha was divided into many states each under a different chief. The chiefs are held together in a confederacy under a peshwa, who is the military and administrative head.

First anglo-maratha war, ended in 1782 with treaty of Salbai, had no clear victory. Second anglo-maratha war (1803-05), British gained orissa and territories of north of yamuna including agra and delhi.Third anglo-maratha war lost by marathas, peshwa was removed.

Claim to paramountcy-

The policy of "paramonutcy" is intitiated under Lord Hastings. The company now claimed that its authority was paramount or supreme. Hence its power was greater than indian states. So to protect company's interest it started annexing or threatning to annex indian kingdoms.

It was challenged by queen of kittor, Rani Channamma. She led an anti- british resistance movement.She was arrested in 1824 and died in 1829. Rayanna, a chowkidar of sangoli in kittor continued the resistance. He was caught in 1830 and hanged.

War in north-west-

In late 1830s, company was worried about rising Russia. To take control of noth-west, compnay fought war with afghanistan between 1838 and 1842. In 1843 they take over on Sindh. In 1849,punjab was annexed.

The doctrine of lapse-

This doctrine declared that if and indian ruler died without a male heir, his kingdom would "lapse".This policy was given by Lord Dalhousie (governer general 1848-1856).

Kingdoms, such as Satara(1848), Sambalpur(1850), Udaipur(1852), Nagpur(1853), Jhansi(1854) were annexed by applying this doctrine.

SETTING UP A NEW ADMINISTRATION

  • British territories were divided into administrative units called presidencies, and there are 3 of them, bengal, madras and bombay. Each presidencies were ruled by governers and the supreme head of administration was governer-general. The first governer general of india was Warren Hastings.
  • A collector was the administative head of a district. The main job of collector was to collect revenue and taxes and to maintain law and order in the district. The office of the collector was called as The collectorate.
  • Each district was to have 2 courts, criminal court(faujdari adalat) and civil court(diwani adalat). This system of justice started in 1772.
  • Hindu laws are compliled in 1775 and muslims laws are in 1778. Under the Regulating Act of 1773, Supreme Court was established.

The company army-

The compnay army mostly composed of army of local kingdoms, consists of cavalry and foot soldiers.

In early nineteenth century, british began to develope uniform military culture. Soldiers were increasingly subjected to european style training. It consists of drills and made discipline among soldiers. This created problems because the cast rules and feelings were ignored to build professional soldiers.

CONCLUSION

East india company transformed from a trading company to a territorial power. Steam technology helped british colonial ambition. Steamships reduced journey time, which enabled more britishers and their family members to come to india.

By 1857, the company had direct rule over 78% of indian population and 63% of indian territory. Company also had indirect influence on remaining places, thus virtually controlled whole india.
